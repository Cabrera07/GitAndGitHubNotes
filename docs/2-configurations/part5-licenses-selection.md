## üõ†Ô∏è **When to Choose a License**

### üìÑ **1. MIT License**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose MIT When:**

- ‚úîÔ∏è You want a **simple, permissive license** with minimal restrictions.
- üìö You're creating **libraries, frameworks, or tools** for widespread use, including commercial projects.
- üîì You don't need derivatives to remain open source.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid MIT If:**

- üîí You want derivatives to **stay open source** (consider using **GPL** instead).

---

### üìÑ **2. Apache License 2.0**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose Apache When:**

- üß† **Patent protection** for contributors and users is important.
- üìÑ You want similar flexibility as **MIT** but with stronger **attribution** and **modification documentation** requirements.
- ü§ù You're working on collaborative open-source projects that need legal safeguards.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid Apache If:**

- üìë You don't want to include a **NOTICE** file for attribution or find it excessive for smaller projects.

---

### üîó **3. GPL (v2)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose GPL (v2) When:**

- üîí You want to **enforce open-source** by requiring all derivatives to remain under **GPLv2** (strong copyleft).
- ‚öôÔ∏è You're working on a project that already uses **GPLv2-licensed code** for compatibility.
- üìú Your project doesn't require modern legal clarifications or patent protections.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid GPL (v2) If:**

- üß† You need **explicit patent protection** (consider **GPLv3**).
- üö´ You want to address modern hardware restrictions like **Tivoization** (use **GPLv3**).
- üîÑ Compatibility with permissive licenses (e.g., **MIT**, **Apache**) is necessary.

---

### üîó **4. GPL (v3)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose GPL (v3) When:**

- üîí You want **strong copyleft** enforcement to ensure all derivatives remain open source.
- üß† You need **explicit patent protections** for contributors and users.
- üö´ Preventing hardware restrictions (**Tivoization**) on modifying your software is important.
- üåê You're working on a modern project requiring legal clarity and global compatibility.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid GPL (v3) If:**

- üîÑ Your project depends on **GPLv2-only code**, as upgrading to **GPLv3** can cause compatibility issues.
- ‚ú® You want a **simpler license** without detailed clauses like patent protection or anti-Tivoization.
- üíº Broad **business adoption** is a primary goal, as some companies may avoid **GPLv3** due to its stricter terms.

---

### üìö **5. LGPL (Lesser General Public License)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose LGPL When:**

- üîó You're building a **library or framework** for integration with proprietary software (allows dynamic linking).
- üåç You want to encourage adoption while keeping the library itself open source.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid LGPL If:**

- üîí You don't want any proprietary use of your work (use **GPL** instead).

---

### üåê **6. AGPL (Affero General Public License)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose AGPL When:**

- üîí You want **strong copyleft protections**, including for **network use** (e.g., SaaS or APIs).
- ‚òÅÔ∏è You're creating **server-side** or **cloud software** where modifications might be hidden otherwise.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid AGPL If:**

- üîì You don't need to enforce open sourcing of network-deployed software.
- üíº You want broader adoption; **AGPL** can deter businesses.

---

### üìÑ **7. BSD (2-Clause)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose BSD (2-Clause) When:**

- ‚úîÔ∏è You need a **simple and permissive license** with minimal restrictions.
- üîó You want to allow **integration into proprietary software**.
- üéì The project is for **academic, research, or lightweight open-source purposes**.
- ‚ú® You don't need to restrict the use of contributor names for promotion.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid BSD (2-Clause) If:**

- üõ°Ô∏è You want to **protect contributors' names** from being used for promotional purposes (use **BSD 3-Clause** instead).
- üîí You need **strong copyleft protections** to ensure derivatives remain open source (consider **GPL**).

---

### üìÑ **8. BSD (3-Clause)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose (3-Clause) When:**

- ‚úîÔ∏è You need a **permissive license** but want to protect **contributors' names** from being used in promotions without permission (via the "no endorsement" clause).
- üîó You want flexibility similar to **2-Clause BSD** while safeguarding contributor reputation.
- üë• Your project involves **multiple contributors** or organizations.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid (3-Clause) If:**

- ‚ú® You want a **simpler licensing option** without the "no endorsement" clause (use **BSD 2-Clause** instead).
- üîí You need **strong copyleft protections** to enforce open sourcing of derivatives (use **GPL**).

---

### ‚öôÔ∏è **9. MPL (Mozilla Public License)**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose MPL When:**

- üîÑ You want a balance between **permissive** and **strong copyleft** licenses (file-level copyleft).
- ‚öôÔ∏è You're working on a project where modifications to specific files must stay open source, but proprietary integration is acceptable.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid MPL If:**

- üîí You need full copyleft enforcement across an entire project (consider **GPL**).

---

### üìÑ **10. ISC License**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose ISC When:**

- ‚úîÔ∏è You want maximum simplicity with permissive licensing (similar to **MIT** but even simpler).
- üìö You're developing lightweight tools, academic projects, or reusable components.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid ISC If:**

- üß† You need patent protections or more robust attribution requirements.

---

### üåê **11. Unlicense**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose When:**

- üåç You want to release your software into the **public domain** with no restrictions.
- üß™ You're creating **experimental, educational, or demonstration projects**.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid If:**

- üìù You want **attribution**, legal protections, or control over derivatives.

---

### üîÑ **12. Dual Licensing**

---

#### ‚úÖ **Choose Dual Licensing When:**

- ü§ù You're building a mature project where open-source community involvement and commercial monetization are both important.
- üíº You need flexibility to license differently for free users and paying customers.

#### ‚ùå **Avoid Dual Licensing If:**

- ‚öñÔ∏è You're not ready to handle the complexity of managing multiple licenses.

---

### üåü **Key Takeaways**

---

- **Assess Your Project's Goals:** Determine whether you prioritize openness, adoption, legal protections, or commercialization.
- **Understand License Implications:** Be aware of how each license affects derivatives, contributions, and business use.
- **Consider Community and Contributors:** Choose a license that aligns with your contributors' expectations and the community's norms.
- **Legal Consultation:** For complex projects or when in doubt, consult with a legal professional to choose the most appropriate license.
